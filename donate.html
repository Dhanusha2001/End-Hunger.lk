Seeds and Skills for Women to Grow Vegetables


We supply vegetable seeds and support to elderly women so they can grow
food! Women farmers produce more than half of the food produced in the
developing countries, despite owning less than 2% of the land and receiving
little help in the form of seeds, tools, and education. We join women's
gardening initiatives in some of the world's poorest countries, such as
Madagascar, Guatemala, Liberia, and Kenya.

Support Small Farmers in India


Seva Mandir aims to assist small farmers and their families in rural parts of
Rajasthan, India, by increasing their livelihoods and food and nutritional
consumption. Farmers will be provided with high-quality seeds, fruit orchids,
and vegetable plantations, as well as capacity-building training in
agricultural techniques, as part of this project. Water Harvesting Structures
will be built and repaired as part of the project to
increase the availability of water for agriculture.

Emergency Food Distribution in Madagascar


COVID-19's severe food shortages and economic effects, along with a severe
climate-induced drought in southern Madagascar, have left over 1.14 million
people in dire straits. Women and children are more vulnerable to hunger,
contributing to life-threatening increases in malnutrition rates. The
Emergency Food Distribution Program of SEED has been working to improve
the immediate health and long-term resilience of food
poor communities since February 2021.

Water for All: pedal pumps and wells for families


We wish to purchase and promote the use of manual pedal pumps in the
Mangochi district's communities. Malawi is one of Africa's poorest countries,
having a high incidence of malnourished children. Pedal water pumps are
an essential tool for agriculture, allowing the hamlet to cope with food
poverty. The second issue to address is the lack of access to safe drinking
water: digging wells in villages will aid families in
preventing the spread of cholera.
