Across the world, up to 811 million people do not have enough food. According to recent estimates, 45 million people in 43 countries are on the verge of famine, the most severe form of hunger that can lead to death through starvation or disease. Parts of Yemen, South Sudan, and Madagascar may be on the verge of famine or have already succumbed to it.


Diets deficient in vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients are hurting the health and well-being of millions of people, as well as casting a pall over the futures of communities and countries.


Despite the fact that enough food is produced to feed everyone on the planet, the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development's goal of a world without hunger, as outlined in Sustainable Development Goal 2, remains a huge challenge due to a toxic cocktail of conflict, climate change, disasters, structural poverty, and inequality. The COVID-19 pandemic's socio-economic implications have aggravated the situation in the last year, pushing millions of vulnerable people into increasing food insecurity.
